NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, 4-digit 5412 contains four 6-digit codes: 541211 CPAs (license-gated, PE-saturated), 541213 Tax Preparation (seasonal cyclicality limits scale), 541214 Payroll (ADP/Paychex/Gusto/Rippling at scale), 541219 Other Accounting Services — the open sub-vertical for bookkeeping + fractional CFO + non-CPA accounting.

Counter-thesis — what would weaken the sub-vertical

Threats to monitor

  • Ramp / Brex / Stripe Atlas / Mercury embedding native bookkeeping + CFO-lite tools in payment stacks at near-zero marginal cost — threatens the $99 bookkeeping entry tier
  • Talent retention through ownership change — fractional CFOs are senior generalists with portable client relationships; 47% Y1 / 75% Y3 attrition norm without golden handcuffs
  • Operator productivity claims (+40-60% per CFO seat) are 2-3× above McKinsey services-AI baseline of +10-20% — at +20%, MoIC drops from 2.8-3.3× to 2× range
  • CPA consolidators move down-market into 541219 if 541211 deal flow slows — would compress the 4-6× entry window

What is honest about the data

  • $156B US accounting market figure is IBISWorld estimate, not primary government data
  • +40-60% productivity per CFO seat is operator-claimed; not verified by published study
  • "Benchmark database 2,200+ firms / $13.25B" type claims are operator-stated, not auditable
  • Tier-2 entry multiples 4-6× are industry consensus; no primary published multiple data for sub-$1M EBITDA accounting tuck-ins

What would have to be true to invest

  • Pipeline of 3-5 boutique 541219 targets at $1-3M revenue with verifiable EBITDA 15-25% and audited customer retention >85%
  • Operator with month-by-month per-CFO seat productivity data showing +20-40% gain post-AI deployment (not just headline target)
  • Underwrite at +20% productivity (McKinsey baseline) for base case; if pilot delivers +40-60%, upside
  • Vertical specialization on trades / healthcare practices / restaurants (not VC-backed startups — Ramp/Brex disintermediation risk)
  • Golden-handcuff retention structures for top CFOs at every acquired firm
