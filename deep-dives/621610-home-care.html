Why the sub-vertical is Conditional, not Strong

Structural constraints on the work itself

  • Clinical labor is not AI-replaceable — the deliverable is hands-on visits (wound care, IV, therapy, bathing)
  • Reimbursement is CMS rate-capped under PDGM; operators cannot price out of margin compression
  • Margin levers are back-office (billing, eligibility, scheduling, prior-auth) — real but capped at single-digit pp
  • Payor-mix shift from Medicaid (5%) to Medicare (10-15%) requires CMS certification, multi-state regulatory work; not capital-gated

Exit ceiling is the binding constraint

  • Enhabit at 1.05× revenue / 10.2× EBITDA in May 2026 is the freshest direct comp
  • Hinge Health IPO May 2025 ~3.5-4× revenue (now lower); Omada Health IPO June 2025 ~3-3.5× revenue — these are tech-enabled health-services comps and still below 5-7× revenue claims in operator IMs
  • 22% Adj EBITDA target requires Medicare conversion + Hospital-at-Home + AI + ACO shared-savings ALL delivering — top-decile outlier outcome
  • Realistic 2028 exit: $300M revenue × 15% EBITDA = $45M EBITDA × 10-12× = $450-540M EV (1.5-1.8× revenue)

What would have to be true to invest

  • Pure-play Medicare-conversion thesis (Medicaid-only agencies that already hold Medicare licenses) with state-by-state pipeline mapped before close
  • Underwrite at Enhabit comp (1.05-1.8× revenue / 10-12× EBITDA), not at 5-7× IM aspiration
  • Operator with audited financials, demonstrated payor-mix shift on at least one acquired agency, and signed ACO contracts (not letters of interest)
  • Entry multiple at $0.20-0.40× revenue (matching observed distress acquisitions of Medicaid-only HHAs), not at $0.50-1× market norm
  • Bias toward smaller positions: this is a Conditional vertical with a Strong sub-thesis at best, not a flagship platform
