NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, 4-digit 5242 contains 524210 Insurance Agencies and Brokerages + 524291 Claims Adjusting + 524292 Third-Party Administration + 524298 All Other Insurance Related Activities. 524210 is the dominant sub-code by firm count and revenue; the 52429 series has weaker characteristics (more commodity, no carrier appointment moat) but does not drag the aggregate below Strong.

Counter-thesis — what would weaken the sub-vertical decision

Threats to monitor

  • PE consolidator pace down-market: 2025 deal count down 11.7% means tier-1 consolidators may move into sub-$1M EBITDA agencies, compressing the 4-6× entry window
  • Carrier change-of-control portability — #1 deal risk per King & Spalding M&A counsel; if carriers tighten consent requirements on PE transactions, integration drag increases materially
  • DTC commercial-lines disintermediation (Next Insurance, Coalition, Embroker) — small-business commercial premium is potentially leak-prone
  • State regulatory drag: 51-state producer licensing slows multi-state platform expansion

Honest cited data we cannot verify

  • Sub-$1M EBITDA tier-2 multiples 4-6× — industry consensus from M&A intermediaries; not primary-sourced (Sica Fletcher SF Index excludes <$1M EBITDA deals)
  • "$317B US HR services" and other broad TAM figures cited in S16 — directional, not primary
  • "Avg principal age 54-55" — Big "I" 2018 Agency Universe; not refreshed in 2024 release
  • ">50% no succession plan" — overstated; actual Big "I" data is "33% expect ownership change in 5 years"
