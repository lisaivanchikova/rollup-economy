Independent P&C Insurance Brokerage

From 4-digit aggregate (5242) into 6-digit specialization (524210) · Tier-2 sub-thesis · Levine Insurance, Thornwood NY operator case
4-digit (5242): Strong → 6-digit (524210): Strong → Tier-2 sub-thesis: Hell-Yes opportunity

Both the 4-digit aggregate (5242 Agencies, Brokerages, and Other Insurance Related Activities) and the 6-digit specialization (524210 Insurance Agencies and Brokerages) score Strong on the main map. They share structural strengths: high-margin commission revenue, recurring premium-driven cash flow, real AI productivity lever, and 87% independent-channel share in commercial lines. Within 524210, the investable opportunity is concentrated in the Tier-2 sub-segment — independent agencies sub-$1M EBITDA / sub-$25M premium volume, where PE consolidator competition is structurally thinner and the multiple arbitrage between entry (4-6× EBITDA) and platform exit (11-12× EBITDA in the >$1M tier) is at its widest in any service-business vertical we track. Levine Insurance (Thornwood NY) — a 2-person, 40-carrier, century-old family agency with zero M&A signal in public sources — is a textbook Tier-2 proprietary target profile.

NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, the 4-digit industry group 5242 Agencies, Brokerages, and Other Insurance Related Activities contains two 5-digit groups, with the following 6-digit codes:

  • 52421 Insurance Agencies and Brokerages → only 6-digit code 524210 — sells/distributes P&C, life, health, benefits. This is where Levine sits.
  • 52429 Other Insurance Related Activities → 524291 Claims Adjusting, 524292 Third-Party Administration, 524298 All Other Insurance Related.

The 524210 sub-segment is what people mean by "insurance brokerages" in common usage. Both 5242 and 524210 score Strong on the main map because they share the same operating characteristics — the 52429 "other" leg (claims/TPA/all other) does not drag the aggregate enough to change the verdict band. The colors match: Strong → Strong.

Why both bands are Strong

Four structural factors carry both the aggregate and the sub-segment:

1. Recurring commission-driven revenue

Standard P&C commission: 10-15% of premium for personal lines, 12-20% for commercial, 15-25%+ for specialty/E&S. Renewal commissions arrive automatically each policy period. The book of business is asset-like — measurable, transferable, financeable.

2. Real AI productivity lever — verified

Hiscox + Google Cloud (Dec 2024 press release) cut London Market specialty quote turnaround from 3 days to 3 minutes (a 99.4% reduction) with 98-99% data ingestion accuracy. McKinsey insurance research cites 10-20% productivity gains for gen-AI users in insurance workflows. The work is mostly information-handling — submission intake, carrier matching, quoting, binding, certificates — and is structurally automatable.

3. Independent channel dominance + growing

87.2% commercial lines independent share in 2024 (Big "I" Market Share Report 2024 on 2023 data); 62.2% of total P&C industry. Independent channel share has expanded steadily over the last decade as the captive model (State Farm, Allstate, Farmers) loses ground in commercial.

4. Fragmentation + succession pressure

39,000 total US independent P&C agencies (Big "I"/Future One 2024 Agency Universe Study). Approximately 30,000 agencies generate under $1.25M annual revenue (OPTIS Partners). Average principal age 54-55 (last published 2018-2022 Agency Universe Studies); ~75% of small agencies will not perpetuate internally (MarshBerry). Supply of acquirable agencies is structurally large and growing.

The within-segment opportunity: Tier-1 vs Tier-2

The PE roll-up window inside 524210 is concentrated at the small end. The top tier is consolidated; the long tail is not.

🔴 Tier-1 (>$1M EBITDA) — closed

Average transaction multiple 11.4-11.9× EBITDA for >$1M EBITDA deals (Sica Fletcher SF Index 2024-2025). Mega-deals trade higher: AssuredPartners $1B EBITDA at ~14.5×; Risk Strategies $600M EBITDA at ~16×. PE-backed buyers placed ~68-72% of transactions in 2024-2025 (MarshBerry / OPTIS). This tier is saturated: 7 named consolidators (Acrisure, Hub, HighStreet, Inszone, Alkeme, King Risk, Keystone Agency Partners) compete actively, multiples are stretched, and entry economics no longer support fund-level returns.

🟢 Tier-2 (sub-$1M EBITDA) — the open window

Estimated transaction multiples 4-6× EBITDA for sub-$1M EBITDA agencies (industry consensus from M&A advisors; not a primary-published statistic — Sica Fletcher's SF Index excludes deals below $1M EBITDA, leaving a transparency gap). Pool of acquirable targets: ~30,000 agencies under $1.25M revenue. The 7 tier-1 consolidators have largely skipped this tier because individual deal economics don't justify their cost-to-close. Multiple arbitrage: 4-6× entry → 11-12× exit when aggregated into a $5M+ EBITDA platform. This is the widest spread we track in any service-business vertical.

Three investable sub-theses within Tier-2

1. Tier-2 retail roll-up (core fund use)

Target profile: $5-25M premium volume · $300-800K EBITDA · 8-20 staff · ≥12 carrier appointments · ≥65% commercial mix · single-state start NY/FL/TX/OH/PA · owner aged 55+. Acquire 8-12 such agencies over 3-4 years; integrate onto shared back-office + AI submission stack; exit aggregated platform at >$5M EBITDA into the tier-1 multiple band. Levine Insurance is one such profile.

2. Carrier-appointment aggregator

Acquire one appointment-rich anchor first (40+ carriers like Levine), then plug tuck-ins under the anchor's appointments. Eliminates the slowest part of M&A — carrier consent and appointment portability negotiation. Acrisure historically followed a version of this pattern before pivoting to brand unification.

3. Embedded-native MGA (venture sleeve only)

Separate from the core fund roll-up — a small SAFE check into a venture-stage embedded insurance / AI-native MGA. Not the primary thesis; tracks the alternative distribution channel that could disrupt the long tail over 5-10 years. Sized as venture sleeve, not roll-up capital.

Top risks

R1 — Carrier appointment portability through ownership change

The single largest deal risk. Nearly every carrier contract contains a Change of Control Clause; carriers can deny continuation or terminate post-close. King & Spalding: "Diligence review of every carrier agreement is not optional; it is a prerequisite." Mitigation: pre-close carrier consent process; selecting agencies with portable appointment structures; legal carve-outs in deal structure. Kill criteria: appointment portability <70% retention through ownership change.

R2 — 51-state regulatory drag

Producer licensing required state-by-state. Multi-state platform must maintain producer licenses, surplus lines licenses (E&S), and continuing-education compliance across every state of operation. Slows platform expansion; adds compliance G&A. Mitigation: single-state start, geographic expansion only as integration playbook proven.

R3 — PE saturation creep down-market

The 7 tier-1 consolidators may move further down-market as their primary deal pool thins (2025 saw deal-count down 11.7% YoY). If they begin actively pursuing sub-$1M EBITDA agencies, the 4-6× tier-2 entry multiple compresses. Mitigation: speed of acquisition; proprietary sourcing (agencies invisible to M&A intermediaries like Levine); focus on geographies less attractive to national consolidators.

R4 — Post-rollup integration friction

Acrisure was sued in 2024-25 by Steven Herrig (SUNZ) over restrictive covenants in a post-sale operating arrangement (court denied TRO); Acrisure's 2023 brand-unification strategy retired local agency identities. Customer complaints about Hub International post-acquisition service quality are extensive (BBB, WalletHub). Average M&A producer attrition runs 47% in year one, 75% by year three (general M&A benchmark; not insurance-specific). Mitigation: retain key producers with golden handcuffs + ongoing commission share; phased branding/integration.

Final thesis

4-digit and 6-digit verdicts match: 5242 Strong → 524210 Strong. Both carry on the same structural strengths — recurring commission revenue, real AI productivity lever (verified Hiscox 99.4% quote cycle reduction; McKinsey 10-20% per-agent productivity), 87% independent share in commercial lines, and ~30,000 long-tail acquisition targets under $1.25M revenue. Within 524210, the investable window is concentrated in Tier-2 (sub-$1M EBITDA, sub-$25M premium), where multiple arbitrage between entry (4-6×) and platform exit (11-12× in >$1M tier per Sica Fletcher) is the widest in any service-business vertical tracked. Levine Insurance (Thornwood NY) is a textbook Tier-2 proprietary target: 2-person family agency, 40 carrier appointments (more than 2× industry average), century-old, completely invisible in public M&A databases. The deep dive sub-thesis is exit-anchored — aggregate 8-12 Tier-2 agencies over 3-4 years into a $5M+ EBITDA platform; exit into the 11-12× tier-1 multiple band where 7 named consolidators (Acrisure, Hub, HighStreet, Inszone, Alkeme, King Risk, Keystone) compete. Primary risk is carrier change-of-control portability through ownership change — addressable in diligence, not structural.
