Enter this sub-vertical with disciplined execution. 5419 Other Professional / Scientific / Technical Services scores Conditional on the main map (ΣS=19,760) — the aggregate lumps veterinary with marketing research, photography, translation, and miscellaneous specialty services with no shared economics. 541940 Veterinary Services is structurally different: healthcare-frequency recurring revenue (~2.4 vet visits per pet per year per AVMA), no payor-mix complexity (cash + pet-insurance pay-direct, avoiding the CMS rate-cap problem of 621610 home health), real AI productivity lever on back-office workflows + FDA-cleared diagnostic AI (radiology, lab, dental imaging), and persistent fragmentation (~32,000 US practices with PE only rolled up roughly 25-30% over the last 5-10 years). The main map data file encodes NAICS_C[541940] = 8 (vs raw 5419 c=2) explicitly recognizing the sub-vertical's structural strength. The +1 band upgrade Conditional → Strong is honest. NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, 4-digit 5419 contains 541910 Marketing Research, 541921 + 541922 Photography, 541930 Translation, 541940 Veterinary Services, 541990 All Other. The aggregate is held down by photography, translation, and "other" — commodity services with weak moats. 541940 is structurally different (recurring healthcare-frequency, no payor-mix complexity, real AI lever) and the main map data file recognizes this via the C=8 override.

Counter-thesis — why Strong, not Hell-Yes

Reasons the +2 band upgrade to Hell-Yes is NOT warranted

  • The Hell-Yes computation depends entirely on the NAICS_C[541940] = 8 override; we are rediscovering a framework-encoded assumption rather than independently deriving it
  • "Third-option" entry-multiple discount (5-8× vs PE 18-20×) is real but time-limited — PE consolidators (JAB Holdings, Mars Veterinary Health, Pets at Home) will pivot toward founder-friendly positioning once they see Positive Holdings winning deals on alignment, compressing the window to ~2-3 years
  • First-time veterinary consolidator execution risk — no operator-led "third-option" platform has yet scaled to $50M+ EBITDA in vet specifically
  • Customer (pet owner) sensitivity to brand consolidation visible in 2024 NYT + WSJ coverage of PE squeeze — backlash extends to all corporate ownership, not just PE

What is honestly Strong (not Hell-Yes)

  • Recurring healthcare-frequency revenue is real and durable across operator changes
  • AI lever on back-office (booking, billing, inventory) + FDA-cleared diagnostic AI is real but capped at single-digit pp EBITDA uplift
  • Pet humanization macro tailwind is real and verified (Surgeon General, NAPHIA)
  • End-of-life sub-segment is genuinely under-served but $2B IM aspirations are unsupported by primary comp data (Service Corporation International took 60+ years to reach ~$10B EV)

What would have to be true to invest

  • Pipeline of 5-10 founder-vet sellers at 5-8× EBITDA with signed LOIs, vets retaining 40-50% equity
  • Operator with named Chief Integration Officer (not first-time consolidator dependent on PE partner expertise alone)
  • Specialty focus (companion-animal mid-market metro practices) rather than dilution across exotic / equine / large-animal
  • Underwrite at 12-14× exit multiple (mid-market platform band, not 18-20× PE-platform pre-burnout)
  • End-of-life startup treated as separate venture investment, not as a $2B platform underwriting
  • Time window ≤3 years — PE pivot to founder-friendly positioning will compress the entry-multiple discount
