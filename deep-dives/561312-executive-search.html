Executive Search Services

From 4-digit aggregate (5613) into 6-digit specialization (561312) · Cyborg HR sub-thesis · Glocap operator case
4-digit (5613): Strong → 6-digit (561312): Strong → Cyborg HR sub-thesis: Hell-Yes opportunity

Both the 4-digit aggregate (5613 Employment Services) and the 6-digit specialization (561312 Executive Search Services) score Strong on the main map. They share the same structural drivers — fee-based recurring revenue from retained searches, real AI productivity lever on sourcing and screening (the most time-consuming step), and a highly fragmented operator base with thousands of boutique firms across 561312 alone. Within 561312, the investable opportunity is concentrated in the Cyborg HR sub-thesis — Adam Zoia's framework of using AI internally to lift revenue per recruiter from $500K to $750K (a J-curve metric for PE), then rolling up 25+ boutique mid-market search firms ($10-50M revenue each) into a single platform that compounds the productivity gain. Glocap Search (Adam Zoia's $25M revenue / 16% EBITDA / 25-recruiter firm) is the operator demonstrating the unit economics before the roll-up begins.

NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, the 4-digit industry group 5613 Employment Services contains four 6-digit codes covering very different operating models:

  • 561311 Employment Placement Agencies — high-volume, low-margin contingency placement (résumé screening, candidate forwarding for non-executive roles)
  • 561312 Executive Search Services — retained search for executive and specialist roles; fee = 25-33% of first-year compensation (Glocap's exact category)
  • 561320 Temporary Help Services — staffing/temp labor agencies (Adecco, Kelly Services, Robert Half, ManpowerGroup); pass-through model
  • 561330 Professional Employer Organizations (PEO) — co-employment / outsourced HR (TriNet, Insperity, Justworks)

The four sub-codes have very different economics. 561320 (temp staffing) carries the largest revenue but lowest margin (3-8% EBITDA, pass-through wages). 561330 (PEO) has 30-40% margins on smaller revenue base. 561311 (contingency placement) is the long-tail / commodity end. 561312 (Executive Search) is the high-fee retainer corner: 25-33% of first-year compensation per placed candidate, retained engagement model, deeper client relationships, top-end margins typically 15-20% for boutique firms.

Where Glocap sits. Glocap Search is a boutique mid-market executive search firm — placed in 561312, not 561311. Their revenue model is retainer-based engagement with private equity and financial services clients in NYC. ~25 recruiters at ~$500K revenue per recruiter = ~$12.5M direct billings plus operational layer = ~$25M total revenue, 16% EBITDA. This profile (boutique mid-market retained search) is the favorable end of 5613 — and the corner where the AI productivity lever has the most leverage because sourcing and screening are 60%+ of recruiter time.

Why both bands are Strong

1. Fee-based recurring revenue

Retained-search model: 25-33% of first-year compensation per executive placed. Repeat engagement with same clients across multiple searches creates de-facto recurring revenue. Glocap-style boutiques typically have 50-70% of revenue from repeat clients within 24 months.

2. Real AI productivity lever — sourcing + screening

60-70% of recruiter time is sourcing (LinkedIn Recruiter searches, profile evaluation, candidate database management) and initial screening. This is the layer where AI compresses the most. Glocap pilot data: Peter Kelly cold outreach response rate jumps from 3% without signal to 30% with signal. Cyborg target: per-recruiter productivity 5×+ (7 leads/day → 50 leads/day) and revenue per recruiter $500K → $750K.

3. Massive fragmentation

US executive search market is highly fragmented. Top firms (Korn Ferry, Heidrick & Struggles, Spencer Stuart, Egon Zehnder, Russell Reynolds) hold meaningful share at the C-suite top end, but the boutique mid-market ($10-50M revenue firms) is thousands of operators wide with no scale consolidator. Adam Zoia's 25+ firm roll-up plan targets exactly this layer.

4. Multiple arbitrage real (services priced on EBITDA)

Boutique mid-market search firms: 4-6× EBITDA. Scaled platform with technology + national footprint: 8-12× EBITDA. Top-tier publics (Korn Ferry KFY): currently ~10-12× EBITDA. The arbitrage between tuck-in entry and platform exit is real, with the AI productivity lift as the value-creation engine on top of multiple expansion.

The Cyborg HR thesis — Adam Zoia's framework

Adam Zoia's stated strategy from the May 2026 conversation with S16VC: "Software sucks, I don't want to sell a software company." The thesis is to use AI internally to lift recruiter productivity, prove the unit economics on Glocap, raise PE capital, then buy 25+ boutique executive-search firms and apply the same playbook at scale.

P1 — Execution layer (sourcing + screening)

Compress the most time-consuming recruiter steps: sourcing on LinkedIn, profile evaluation, initial outreach, screening. Target: hours per search 31 → 15 (~50% reduction). Operator data: cold outreach response rate goes from 3% (no signal) to 30% (with hiring signal targeting). LinkedIn Recruiter remains the primary sourcing platform.

P2 — Origination engine (data-driven BD)

Detect hiring signals automatically: company raised Series A, spike of 15-50 new job postings, revenue growth patterns. Automatically score targets and send personalized outreach. Goal: lift revenue per recruiter from $500K to $750K (+50%) by replacing cold prospecting with signal-driven engagement. This is the J-curve metric for the PE pitch.

P3 — Roll-up scale (25+ firms)

Buy 25+ boutique executive-search firms ($5-50M revenue each). Apply Cyborg productivity stack uniformly. Compound the revenue-per-recruiter gain across the consolidated platform. Standard back-office consolidation: G&A allocated per firm drops from 16% to ~6-8% at scale. Path to $400-500M revenue platform over 4-5 years.

Dual Agentic Model — defensible differentiation

The PE-pitch differentiator: one AI agent represents the candidate, second AI agent represents the client; agents negotiate shortlist using hiring history, team composition, hiring-manager preferences. Frames the deal not as "yet another recruiting platform" but as a new search architecture. Required to justify the J-curve narrative to PE.

Adam's strategic rules — verbatim from operator conversation

Rules that define the playbook

  • Don't approach HR/TA — go to the hiring manager (sales director, founder, CEO). HR gatekeeps; the buyer is the line manager.
  • Pooling beats per-search. One candidate pool sold to 5 clients = fee scales exponentially, not linearly.
  • Outreach length rule: message must fit on iPhone screen without scrolling (under 100 words).
  • Sequence: 3 touches in week 1, decreasing frequency, total 3 weeks. After that the lead is cold.
  • Sourcing: LinkedIn Recruiter only (not GitHub yet — under evaluation).
  • Target roles: Account Executives, Customer Success, GTM, SaaS sales — high-volume, mid-comp where AI matters most.
  • Channel arithmetic: Paraform marketplace takes 15% — used while building direct pipeline; reduce reliance as origination engine matures.

Top risks

R1 — Productivity gain falls short of $500K → $750K target

The +50% revenue per recruiter is the J-curve metric for the PE pitch. If standalone AI productivity lands at +20% (independent estimate, consistent with public McKinsey services-AI data), the platform-scale economics still work but the headline narrative compresses. Diligence: Glocap pilot data on Peter Kelly's per-month conversion through full 2026 before underwriting the J-curve narrative.

R2 — Competitive thesis collision

Pioneers (Leo Shangin), Fonzi AI (Drew Moffitt), and Chassi (Shane Penner) are pursuing variants of the same operator-led AI roll-up thesis. None has scaled yet, but the sourcing pool of acquirable boutique firms is finite and prices will rise if multiple consolidators bid. First-mover advantage matters; the window may be 18-24 months before competitive pricing pressure compresses entry multiples.

R3 — Recruiter retention through ownership change

Executive search is a people-business; top recruiters can walk with their client relationships intact. Roll-up integration risk: golden-handcuff retention agreements required for the productive recruiters at each acquired firm. Without retention, the firm's revenue follows the recruiter, not the entity. Industry-norm: 47% Y1 producer attrition without retention package, 75% by Y3.

R4 — Client concentration in financial services / PE

Glocap's revenue is anchored in PE and financial-services placements. Cycle-sensitive: when PE deal flow drops, search demand drops. The roll-up needs sector diversification away from FS-only client base to reduce cyclicality. Acquisition criteria should target firms with healthcare, technology, or industrials client mix.

Final thesis

4-digit and 6-digit verdicts match: both 5613 and 561312 are Strong — fee-based recurring revenue from retained searches, real AI productivity lever on the most time-consuming recruiter steps (sourcing + screening), massive fragmentation in the boutique mid-market, and multiple arbitrage from EBITDA-priced services. Within 561312, the Cyborg HR sub-thesis opens a Hell-Yes opportunity: Adam Zoia's operator-led roll-up of 25+ boutique executive-search firms using an internally-deployed AI stack to lift revenue per recruiter from $500K to $750K (the J-curve metric for PE) and compress G&A through shared back-office. Glocap Search ($25M revenue, 16% EBITDA, ~25 recruiters in NYC PE/financial-services focus) is the operator validating the unit economics before consolidation begins; Peter Kelly's pilot shows the productivity unlock (3% → 30% cold outreach response with hiring-signal targeting). Conservative independent estimate: standalone Cyborg deployment lifts Glocap to ~21% EBITDA; 10-firm platform reaches ~24% per-firm EBITDA, yielding ~2.7-3.3× MoIC over a 4-5 year hold at 9-11× exit multiple. SeekOut's downround from $1.2B to $435M validates the "software sucks, build the operator" framing — but the competitive set is filling: Pioneers, Fonzi AI, Chassi pursuing variants. The first 18-24 months matter for sourcing advantage. Underwriting works at the Glocap operator data and the boutique-to-platform multiple arbitrage; the +50% productivity target needs Q3-Q4 2026 pilot data before sizing the position to the headline number.
