NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, 4-digit 5613 Employment Services contains four 6-digit codes with very different economics:

  • 561311 Employment Placement Agencies — contingency, 8-12% margin, commodity
  • 561312 Executive Search Services — retained, 15-20% margin (the favorable corner)
  • 561320 Temporary Help — 3-8% margin, pass-through (Adecco/Kelly/Robert Half/ManpowerGroup at scale)
  • 561330 PEO — 30-40% margin but different model (outsourced HR, not recruiting)

561312 is the right sub-vertical for retained-search operator-led roll-up. The 561320 staffing tier is scale-dominated and not investable at our size. 561330 PEO is high-margin but software-led with strong incumbents (TriNet, Insperity, Justworks).

Counter-thesis — what would weaken the sub-vertical

Threats to monitor

  • Competitive thesis crowding — 4+ operator-led AI roll-ups already articulated for HR; sourcing pool of boutique targets is finite
  • Recruiter retention through ownership change — 47% Y1 attrition / 75% Y3 industry norm without golden handcuffs
  • Operator productivity claims (+50% revenue per recruiter) are 2.5-5× above McKinsey services-AI baseline of +10-20% — execution risk on the headline
  • LinkedIn Recruiter is the single sourcing dependency; any policy change at LinkedIn affects unit economics across the sub-vertical

What the operator pitch removes if conservative

  • At +20% productivity (not +50%), revenue per recruiter $500K → $600K — meaningfully smaller J-curve
  • At +20% productivity, platform-scale EBITDA ~21% (not 24-28%) — MoIC drops to 2× range from 2.7-3.3×
  • Tier-1 strategic buyer concentration (Korn Ferry, Heidrick & Struggles, Spencer Stuart, Egon Zehnder, Russell Reynolds) is the exit channel; if they don't pay for AI narrative, exit multiple compresses

What would have to be true to invest

  • Operator pilot data showing 30-50 leads/day per recruiter actually achieved (not just pipeline targets) — the +50% productivity headline needs verification before sizing
  • Pipeline of 3-5 boutique targets at $5-25M revenue with 16%+ EBITDA, signed LOIs, golden-handcuff structures pre-negotiated
  • Underwrite at boutique 4-6× EBITDA entry / 8-10× platform exit (not 10-12×) — Korn Ferry public multiple is cyclical
  • Specialty focus (PE/financial services, healthcare, technology, GTM/SaaS sales) rather than generalist — concentration discipline reduces commodity-recruiting pricing pressure

Operator question (separate)

Glocap Search (Adam Zoia CEO, NYC; Cyborg HR thesis with Nicolas Bobroff at Lusin) is the operator in this sub-vertical. Operator unit economics solid; productivity headline aggressive.

  • Verified operator data: $25M revenue, 16% EBITDA, ~25 recruiters @ ~$500K each, 2 operationists targeted for AI replacement. Standard 50/50 split structure. EBITDA in line with boutique benchmark.
  • Pilot data (Peter Kelly, joined Jan 2026): $50K/mo revenue, 3 placements/mo, 3% cold outreach response without signal → 30% with hiring-signal targeting (10× lift) — pilot-scale verification of the funnel mechanic
  • Cyborg HR target: revenue per recruiter $500K → $750K (+50% productivity, J-curve metric for PE pitch). McKinsey baseline +10-20%. Adam explicit: "Software sucks, I don't want to sell a software company" — operator-led framing correct given SeekOut downround
  • Competitive collision: Pioneers (Leo Shangin), Fonzi AI (Drew Moffitt), Chassi (Shane Penner) pursuing variants of same operator-led AI roll-up thesis across services
