YCare (y.care) — Company-Specific Re-Verification

6-digit NAICS 621610 · compared against 4-digit aggregate 6216 · why the verdict color does not change
4-digit (6216): Conditional = 6-digit (621610): Conditional

The verdict color does not change between 4-digit and 6-digit because NAICS 6216 has only one 6-digit child — 621610. Structurally 6216 = 62161 = 621610. There is no hidden sub-segment to upgrade (unlike NAICS 4859 where 485991 NEMT splits out a Strong sub-segment from a Conditional aggregate). The honest analytical contribution at the 6-digit level for YCare is within-code payor-mix sub-segmentation — Medicare-certified skilled HH vs Medicaid attendant care vs at-risk ACO / Hospital-at-Home — not a different NAICS code. YCare's strategic positioning (Medicare Advantage + at-risk ACO corner) is the favorable end of 621610, but it sits inside the same Conditional vertical on our map.

NAICS structure — why 6216 = 621610

The 2022 NAICS classification places Home Health Care Services as a single industry with no sub-codes. The hierarchy is degenerate at the 6-digit level:

62 — Health Care and Social Assistance (sector)
621 — Ambulatory Health Care Services (subsector)
6216 — Home Health Care Services (industry group)
62161 — Home Health Care Services (NAICS industry)
621610 — Home Health Care Services (national industry)
↳ Only one 6-digit child. ~36,000 establishments, ~1.4M employees (US Census 2022).

Compare this to the NEMT case where the 4-digit 4859 (Other Transit and Ground Passenger Transportation) splits into three 6-digit children — 485991 NEMT, 485992 special needs, 485999 all other — and 485991 has a materially better consolidation profile than the aggregate. For YCare, no such structural arbitrage exists. The analytical work has to happen at the payor-mix and care-model layer inside 621610.

Within-621610 sub-segmentation — where YCare actually plays

NAICS 621610 lumps together five economically distinct businesses. The aggregate margin and multiple statistics are blends. YCare's IM positions the platform in the Medicare Advantage and at-risk ACO corner, which has the strongest unit economics inside 621610 but also the steepest execution requirements.

Verdict alignment — why color matches

🟡 6216 (4-digit) — Conditional

Generic home health aggregate. Clinical labor is not AI-replaceable (low V). Hands-on care delivery is not automatable (low A). Reimbursement is regulated and rate-capped. The defensible margin lever is back-office and payor-mix arbitrage, not the underlying service.

🟡 621610 (6-digit) — Conditional (same)

NAICS hierarchy is degenerate: 6216 has only one 6-digit child. The aggregate is the sub-segment. No hidden Strong sub-code exists. Within-code payor-mix variance is real but is intra-code execution edge, not a different vertical on our map.

YCare's specific positioning in the Medicare Advantage / at-risk ACO corner of 621610 is the most favorable execution path inside a Conditional vertical. The acquisition entry math (0.28× revenue for Gifted Health) is genuinely cheap. The platform-allocated margin model (16–17% net at scale, our independent estimate) is achievable. What does not survive public verification: the 5–7× revenue exit multiple, the 22% Adj EBITDA top-line target, the unconfirmed Chrysalis 72-site footprint, and the unverified LVAD-specific UF Health MOU. A disciplined investor can underwrite the Gifted Health unit economics, but should size the position to the 1.5–4× revenue exit range (Enhabit comp ceiling), not the IM's headline.
