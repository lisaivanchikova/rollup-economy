NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, 4-digit 5313 Activities Related to Real Estate contains:

  • 531311 Residential Property Managers — fee-based management for third-party residential owners
  • 531312 Nonresidential Property Managers — same model for office/retail/industrial
  • 531320 Offices of Real Estate Appraisers
  • 531390 Other Activities Related to Real Estate — escrow, fiduciary, listing services

531311 and 531312 carry the Hell-Yes drivers; 531320 + 531390 are weaker but minority of aggregate firm count. The sub-vertical 531311 inherits parent verdict by structure.

Distinction from master-lease lessors (531110). 531311 PM operators collect a fee on rent — landlord retains the lease and the asset. They never carry occupancy risk. This is structurally different from 531110 master-lease operators who sign the lease themselves (Common/Sonder/Selina/Quarters/Bedly all dead). The PM sub-vertical decision does not inherit the master-lease graveyard.

Counter-thesis — what would invalidate

Threats to monitor

  • AppFolio / Buildium / Yardi / Entrata launch credible AI-native products and close the displacement window (12-24 months risk)
  • National giants (Greystar, FirstService, Cushman & Wakefield) move down-market into mid-tier consolidation
  • Multifamily real-estate cycle correction depresses fee base across the segment for several quarters
  • Major federal/state regulatory change on tenant-screening or fair-housing that complicates PM economics

None of the above currently obtain

  • Legacy PM software vendors continue to ship incremental rather than AI-native releases (verifiable via product release notes)
  • Giant tier acquisitions focus on enterprise multifamily (Greystar) or HOA (FirstService) — not SFR/mid-market PM
  • Rental fundamentals remain solid post AVB-EQR merger (May 2026 confirmed expansion)
  • No imminent federal regulation visible in HUD or FHA 2026 agenda
