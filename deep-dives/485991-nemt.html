Deep Dive · Sub-Vertical Decision · NAICS 485991

NEMT — Non-Emergency Medical Transportation

Sub-vertical decision · 6-digit 485991 within 4-digit 4859
Sub-vertical decision: PASS 4859 parent: Pass · 485991 sub: Pass

Do not enter this sub-vertical. The structural case against 485991 NEMT is established by primary evidence: the largest public operator (Modivcare) filed Chapter 11 on Aug 20, 2025 with NEMT segment operating margins compressed to 3.8% Q3 2025; Medicare Advantage transportation benefits cut from 36% to 24% of plans 2024→2026 per KFF; CMS provider enrollment moratorium since Oct 2023; broker market consolidated into a duopoly (MTM + Modivcare-post-Ch11) creating single-buyer exit risk; no peer-reviewed deployment study supports the AI margin uplift required to underwrite. A "selective thesis" (specialty routes + direct MCO contracts + conservative leverage + sub-5-year exit) might theoretically work for one specific operator but the sub-vertical itself does not reward a fund-level position. The right answer at the sub-vertical decision is Pass.

NAICS structure

Per US Census 2022 NAICS, 4-digit 4859 Other Transit and Ground Passenger Transportation contains exactly two 6-digit codes: 485991 Special Needs Transportation (NEMT) and 485999 All Other Transit and Ground Passenger Transportation (employee shuttles, airport shuttles, miscellaneous local transit). The 4859 aggregate scores Pass on the main map. 485991 is structurally better than its 485999 sibling (federal mandate, recurring Medicaid revenue) but does not climb above Pass once the structural headwinds enumerated above are weighed.

Counter-thesis — what would make 485991 investable

None of the following currently obtain

  • Modivcare post-Ch11 returning to 10-15% segment margins (would invalidate the structural-margin-compression thesis)
  • MA plans reversing transportation-benefit cuts (currently moving in wrong direction through at least 2026)
  • Peer-reviewed NEMT deployment study showing >5 pp EBITDA uplift from AI dispatch (does not exist)
  • Second strategic acquirer emerging at MTM's scale (no candidate on horizon)
  • CMS provider moratorium lifting + fraud enforcement easing (current trajectory is the opposite)

What is true regardless of operator choice

  • Reimbursement rates are CMS-controlled; operators cannot price out of compression
  • Clinical content of the trip is non-discretionary but the broker pricing model squeezes margins below the operator level
  • Strategic acquirer pool concentration creates buyer power against the operator at exit
  • Top-line growth in the category requires either Medicaid expansion (politically unstable) or MA supplemental benefit growth (currently reversing)

Operator question (separate from sub-vertical question)

Hybrid Ride, Inc. (Greater Boston, 30 vehicles, $3.5M revenue, 13.3% EBITDA before AI) is a legitimate small NEMT operator with verifiable unit economics — wage rates match BLS OEWS, reimbursement matches MassHealth 101 CMR 327.03 ($40.55 wheelchair base trip). The operator data is real. The operator is not the problem. The sub-vertical is. A good operator in a Pass sub-vertical does not become a Strong investment.
