Methodology

Vertical opportunity map for AI-driven PE roll-ups · 308 NAICS sub-sectors × 13 APQC business process categories
We score industry opportunities at the level of cells — each cell is one NAICS sub-sector intersected with one APQC business process category. A cell scores well when five conditions co-occur: (1) AI actually dislodges value in this process, (2) the operator can capture that value rather than lose it to a customer or upstream gatekeeper, (3) the market adopts AI within the fund's investment horizon, (4) targets are acquirable at sane multiples, and (5) the buy-to-exit multiple arbitrage is wide enough to make roll-up math work. A sub-sector is Hell-Yes when at least two of its 13 cells score in the top tier.

The formula

Scell = V × C × A × B × M Cell score = Value Created × Capture Share × Adoption Speed × Buyability × Multiple Arbitrage

Two layers

Layer 1 — Sub-sector cards. Each card aggregates the 13 process-category cells of one NAICS sub-sector. A sub-sector qualifies as Hell-Yes if at least two cells score ≥ 12,000, or one cell ≥ 12,000 and total Σ S ≥ 50,000. Strong: max cell ≥ 12,000 or Σ S ≥ 32,400. Conditional: Σ S ≥ 12,000. Pass / KILL below that.

Layer 2 — Business process matrix. Answers the operational question: once we acquire an operator in a Hell-Yes sub-sector, which processes should be automated first? A vertical can be Hell-Yes overall but red in specific categories (Property Management is consistently red on External Relationships and consistently green on Financial Resources — that drives the post-acquisition playbook).

Why multiplication, not summation

Summation would let a strong V compensate for a weak C. That's the mortgage processors trap: V is huge (banks automate claims, save hundreds of millions per year), but C ≈ 0 — five banks own the borrower relationship, every saved dollar flows upstream to them. Sum would falsely greenlight this. Multiplication punishes any weak component: 9 × 9 × 9 × 0.5 = 364 (Pass), not 27 (which a sum would give).

The four multipliers

VValue Created — what AI dislodged

What
$/year that AI displaces from incumbent in this cell (NAICS × APQC category) nationally. Normalized to 0–10 scale.
Formula
V = V_base × workflow_modifier × applicability
V_base
Sub-sector labor intensity × AI replaceability. Each NAICS 4-digit gets a 0–10 base score from BLS QCEW labor share and Anthropic Economic Index task replaceability.
Workflow mod
Per-APQC-category coefficient (0.25-1.00) reflecting how exposed each process category is to GenAI replacement. Financial Resources = 1.00 (accounting / treasury / reporting are highly replaceable). Customer Service = 0.95 (tier-1 chatbots). External Relationships = 0.25 (relationship work). Vision & Strategy = 0.25 (judgment work). Calibrated against the Anthropic Economic Index task usage distribution.
Applicability
Some categories don't apply to all sectors. "4. Deliver Physical Products" doesn't apply to pure services (54x, 53x, 81x, 92x). "2. R&D" doesn't apply to commodity (21, 42, 44, 45) or government.
Sources
BLS QCEW 2024 for industry labor share. Anthropic Economic Index (March 2026) for AI productivity per occupation, weighted to NAICS via BLS OEWS crosswalk. APQC PCF v7.4 Cross-Industry for the process taxonomy.
Why V exists: if AI didn't dislodge value (V near 0), the cell is unattractive. BPO × "6. Customer Service" has V=10 (AI removes 90% of agents) — but C zeroes it because value flows to direct-to-enterprise AI vendors, not BPO operators.

CCapture Share — what we pocket

What
% of V we keep (not upstream gatekeeper, generic-AI vendor, or regulator). 0–10 scale = 0.0–1.0 × 10.
Formula
C = 10 × π_dist × π_moat
π_dist
Distribution Control ∈ [0, 1]. 0 = upstream gatekeeper owns customer (banks in mortgage; OEMs in parts; ANGI/Thumbtack aggregators in handyman). 1 = operator owns customer directly (broker in insurance, agency in HomeCare, property manager in PM).
π_moat
Moat strength ∈ [0, 1]. License + proprietary data + physical presence + multi-sided network. 0 = generic AI does it for 12 months without us. 1 = regulated license + 20yr client history + physical service.
Sources
Damodaran NYU Stern sector margins. Industry-specific benchmarks: AICPA / CPA.com CAS Benchmark 2024, MarshBerry Top 100 Insurance 2024, KeyBanc SaaS Survey 2024, Service Leadership Index Q4 2024, ADA Dental Practice Survey 2024, CMS NHE, AVMA Veterinary Industry Tracker, Buildium PM 2024, NRA Restaurant Profitability, ATA / ATRI Trucking 2025, SIA Staffing Gross Margin 2025.
Why C is multiplicative inside itself: distribution and moat are both necessary. Strong distribution + no moat → generic AI bypasses us within a year. Strong moat + weak distribution → value flows to whoever owns customer. Multiplication closes both holes.

AAdoption Speed — how fast market accepts

What
Speed of AI adoption in this category × sub-sector, in years until 50% market adoption. Faster = more $/year extracted before commoditization equalizes margins.
Formula
A = 10 / t_50
Calibration
t_50 = 1 yr → A = 10 (doc parsing in claims, already happening). t_50 = 2 yr → A = 5 (most back-office). t_50 = 5 yr → A = 2 (RN scheduling in HomeCare: cultural + regulatory drag). t_50 = 10 yr → A = 1 (surgery, litigation, strategic decisions).
Sources
Composite of: Anthropic Economic Index (March 2026) — 30%, McKinsey MGI GenAI 2024 + Goldman Sachs GenAI 2023 exposure — 25%, O*NET 28.0 routine task share — 15%, industry surveys (NAHC, AICPA, CompTIA / Kaseya, MarshBerry, ADA, AMA, IAB, AIA, NRA, ATA Tech Council) — 15%, BLS Employment Projections 2024-34 — 10%, Frey-Osborne — 5%.
Why A: Total fund value = ($/year captured) × (years in window). If adoption is slow, the value window opens after the fund's exit horizon. A high-V cell with slow A cannot deliver returns within IC tolerance.

BBuyability — can we enter

What
Whether the fund can actually acquire operators: enough targets in the $1-10M EBITDA band, willing owners, disciplined entry pricing, manageable competition from other PE platforms.
Formula
B = 10 × (TD × OW × PD) / (1 + CFD)
TD
Target Density: log(N operators in our $1–10M EBITDA band). Not "thousands", but "how many in our band".
OW
Owner Willingness: silver tsunami × succession gap × economic distress. 60+ owner without succession + distressed = high.
PD
Price Discipline: (Public comp − Asking) / Public comp. Positive = we buy below comp.
CFD
Competition For Deals: log(1 + active PE platforms).
B = 0
For Government (sector 92), Postal Service (4911), Central Bank (5211), Management of Companies (5511), Religious Orgs (8131), Private Households (8141). These auto-KILL — not acquirable.
Why B is multiplicative across TD, OW, PD: A vertical with high fragmentation but no willing sellers (TD high, OW low) is not acquirable. A single-target opportunity at the right price with no competing bidders (TD low, OW high, CFD low) still passes — fragmentation alone is not a sufficient gate.

MMultiple Arbitrage Spread — buy low, sell high

What
The spread between the buy multiple paid for sub-$3M EBITDA tuck-in acquisitions (typically 3-5×) and the exit multiple realized when the consolidated $10M+ EBITDA platform is sold (typically 10-15×). This is the core PE roll-up economics: without spread, roll-up math does not work even when AI fit is perfect.
Formula
M = round((exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult × 4), capped 0-10
Examples
Insurance Brokers P&C (4-11× → 14-19×) = M 9. MSP / IT Services (4-6× → 9-14×) = M 8. HomeCare (3-6× → 10-14×) = M 7. Accounting (3-5.5× → 7-12×) = M 7. SaaS (6-8× → 10-12×, compressed) = M 3. Dental DSO (5-9× → 10-12×, saturated) = M 4. Commercial banks (priced on P/TBV, not EBITDA) = M 2. Real Estate Lessors (asset-priced on cap rate) = M 2.
Sources
Capstone Partners Middle Market 2025, Mertz Taggart Q1 2026 (Home-Based Care), MarshBerry Insurance 2024, Sica Fletcher 2025, FOCUS Bankers Healthcare 2026, Aventis MSP 2025, Kroll HVAC 2025, PKF Summer 2025, Pepperdine PCMR 2025, Bain Global PE Report 2026, Houlihan Lokey T&L Q2 2025.
Why M is necessary: A vertical can have perfect AI fit (V = 10) and still deliver zero MoIC if buy and exit multiples are equal — productivity gains get repriced by customers (the Klarna pattern in BPO). M is the discipline that separates "where AI applies" from "where PE roll-up math works".

Why these thresholds — geometric mean derivation

Thresholds are derived from the geometric mean of the five blocks. The cell score S is a product of 0-10 dimensions; the natural cutoff is "all dimensions at least good" (geom mean ≥ 6.5). Hell-Yes corresponds to 6.5⁵ ≈ 11,600 (rounded to 12,000); the lower tiers step down to "all dimensions average" (5⁵ = 3,125) and "all dimensions weak but non-zero" (3⁵ ≈ 170).

The formula S = V × C × A × B with each block 0–10 has a mathematical structure that makes the geometric mean (= "average dimension score") the natural threshold rule:

ScenarioPer-block scoreS = score⁴
Maximum1010,000
All "excellent"96,561
All "good"72,401
All "average"5625
All "weak"381
All "bad"11

If we define Hell-Yes = "all four blocks at least good (≥7)" — meaning no dimension is a weakness — then the threshold is 7⁴ = 2,401. Round to 2,400.

Same logic for other tiers:

  • Strong = all blocks ≥ 5 (average or better) → 5⁴ = 625
  • Conditional = all blocks ≥ 3 (weak but non-zero) → 3⁴ = 81 (round to 100)
  • Pass = geom mean ≥ 1.5 → 1.5⁴ ≈ 5 (round to 20)
  • KILL = at least one block near zero → below 20

Why geometric mean, not arithmetic: the formula is multiplicative. Arithmetic mean would let one strong block compensate for a near-zero block (8+8+8+0 → mean 6). Geometric mean correctly penalizes weak links (0⁴ = 0 regardless of others). This is the right math for "no link in chain is weak".

Why this is better than anchoring: the threshold is now reproducible and defensible. Anyone running the formula on a new dataset gets the same answer about what "Hell-Yes" means. We don't need to recalibrate when the dataset shifts.

Vertical-level verdicts (Layer 1)

For aggregating cell scores up to vertical level:

  • Hell-Yes vertical = ≥2 Hell-Yes cells, OR ≥1 Hell-Yes cell + Σ S ≥ 10,000
  • Strong vertical = max cell ≥ 2,400, OR Σ S ≥ 6,500
  • Conditional vertical = Σ S ≥ 2,400
  • Pass vertical = Σ S ≥ 625
  • KILL vertical = below

Worked example

Insurance Brokerage P&C (NAICS 5242) × "9. Manage Financial Resources"

V = 7V_base 7 (high labor intensity, regulated docs) × workflow_modifier 1.0 (Financial Resources highest AI exposure). LR ≈ $3.6B/yr nationally (claims doc parsing κ=0.95, AR/AP automation).
C = 7π_dist = 0.85 (broker owns client; carrier doesn't). π_moat = 0.85 (50-state license + accumulated book of business). C = 10 × 0.85 × 0.85 ≈ 7.
A = 6t_50 ≈ 1.7 yrs: high adoption pressure (labor savings obvious), moderate friction (compliance), current adoption ~20%.
B = 7tier-2 segment: 145K agencies, low PE saturation (PE prefers >$5M EBITDA), strong silver tsunami, median asking 5.5×, public comps 11×.
S = 7 × 7 × 6 × 7 = 2,058 — Strong 🟢
