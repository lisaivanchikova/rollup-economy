Methodology — AI Value Migration v3.0

Full US economy · NAICS 4-digit × APQC PCF Level 1 · context-free · 2026-05-23
In one paragraph. We don't pick "good industries" — we pick cells (NAICS sub-sector × APQC business process category) where (1) AI dislodged measurable value from an incumbent, (2) we can pocket it instead of an upstream gatekeeper, (3) the market adopts AI within our investment horizon, and (4) we can actually buy a way in. The product of those four conditions = cell score. Vertical-level verdict = aggregate of cell scores: a sub-sector is "Hell-Yes" if ≥2 cells score in Hell-Yes range. Sum of all green cells = the fund's total addressable opportunity.

The formula

Scell = V × C × A × B Cell score = Value Created × Capture Share × Adoption Speed × Buyability

Two layers

Layer 1 — Sub-sector cards (NAICS 4-digit) answer the executive question: "Should we invest in this vertical?" Each card aggregates all 13 APQC process cells in that sub-sector. Thresholds are derived from geometric mean of V×C×A×B (see "Why these thresholds" section below) — Hell-Yes if ≥2 cells score ≥2,400 or ≥1 cell ≥2,400 + Σ S ≥ 10,000; Strong if max cell ≥ 2,400 or Σ S ≥ 6,500; Conditional if Σ S ≥ 2,400; Pass / KILL below.

Layer 2 — APQC process category matrix answers the operational question: "After we acquire an operator, which business processes do we automate?" A vertical can be "Hell-Yes" overall but have one specific process category that's red (e.g. Property Mgmt "12. External Relationships" is always red — that's by design; the playbook deploys on Financial Resources, Customer Service, Market & Sell). The matrix shows where AI plays well vs. where it doesn't.

Why multiplication, not summation

Summation would let a strong V compensate for a weak C. That's the mortgage processors trap: V is huge (banks automate claims, save hundreds of millions per year), but C ≈ 0 — five banks own the borrower relationship, every saved dollar flows upstream to them. Sum would falsely greenlight this. Multiplication punishes any weak component: 9 × 9 × 9 × 0.5 = 364 (Pass), not 27 (which a sum would give).

The four multipliers

VValue Created — what AI dislodged

What
$/year that AI displaces from incumbent in this cell (NAICS × APQC category) nationally. Normalized to 0–10 scale.
Formula
V = V_base × workflow_modifier × applicability
V_base
Sub-sector labor intensity × AI replaceability. Each NAICS 4-digit gets a 0–10 base score from BLS QCEW labor share and Anthropic Economic Index task replaceability.
Workflow mod
Per-APQC-category modifier. 1.0 for Financial Resources (highest AI replaceability — Karpathy 9–10/10 for accountants). 0.25 for External Relationships (relationship work). 0.95 for Customer Service (chatbots eat tier-1).
Applicability
Some categories don't apply to all sectors. "4. Deliver Physical Products" doesn't apply to pure services (54x, 53x, 81x, 92x). "2. R&D" doesn't apply to commodity (21, 42, 44, 45) or government.
Sources
BLS QCEW · Anthropic Economic Index Mar-26 · Karpathy.ai/jobs · APQC PCF v7.4 for category taxonomy
Why V exists: if AI didn't dislodge value (V near 0), the cell is unattractive. BPO × "6. Customer Service" has V=10 (AI removes 90% of agents) — but C zeroes it because value flows to direct-to-enterprise AI vendors, not BPO operators.

CCapture Share — what we pocket

What
% of V we keep (not upstream gatekeeper, generic-AI vendor, or regulator). 0–10 scale = 0.0–1.0 × 10.
Formula
C = 10 × π_dist × π_moat
π_dist
Distribution Control ∈ [0, 1]. 0 = upstream gatekeeper owns customer (banks in mortgage; OEMs in parts; ANGI/Thumbtack aggregators in handyman). 1 = operator owns customer directly (broker in insurance, agency in HomeCare, property manager in PM).
π_moat
Moat strength ∈ [0, 1]. License + proprietary data + physical presence + multi-sided network. 0 = generic AI does it for 12 months without us. 1 = regulated license + 20yr client history + physical service.
Sources
Operator interviews · NAIC licensing databases · Vendor failure-mode case studies · Bessemer/Sequoia moat research
Why C is multiplicative inside itself: distribution and moat are both necessary. Strong distribution + no moat → generic AI bypasses us within a year. Strong moat + weak distribution → value flows to whoever owns customer. Multiplication closes both holes.

AAdoption Speed — how fast market accepts

What
Speed of AI adoption in this category × sub-sector, in years until 50% market adoption. Faster = more $/year extracted before commoditization equalizes margins.
Formula
A = 10 / t_50
Calibration
t_50 = 1 yr → A = 10 (doc parsing in claims, already happening). t_50 = 2 yr → A = 5 (most back-office). t_50 = 5 yr → A = 2 (RN scheduling in HomeCare: cultural + regulatory drag). t_50 = 10 yr → A = 1 (surgery, litigation, strategic decisions).
Sources
Anthropic Economic Index (current adoption % as leading indicator) · Historical analogues (cloud 2008→2018; ERP 1995→2005; mobile 2007→2014) · McKinsey AI Adoption Survey
Why A: Nicolas's "$/time" formulation. Total fund $ = ($/year) × (years in window). If adoption is slow, the window opens too late for fund horizon. Even a high-V cell with slow A can't deliver returns within IC tolerance.

BBuyability — can we enter

What
Whether Bobroff can actually acquire operators: enough targets, willing owners, sane multiples, no PE platforms blocking. Replaces "fragmentation as gate".
Formula
B = 10 × (TD × OW × PD) / (1 + CFD)
TD
Target Density: log(N operators in our $1–10M EBITDA band). Not "thousands", but "how many in our band".
OW
Owner Willingness: silver tsunami × succession gap × economic distress. 60+ owner without succession + distressed = high.
PD
Price Discipline: (Public comp − Asking) / Public comp. Positive = we buy below comp.
CFD
Competition For Deals: log(1 + active PE platforms).
B = 0
For Government (sector 92), Postal Service (4911), Central Bank (5211), Management of Companies (5511), Religious Orgs (8131), Private Households (8141). These auto-KILL — not acquirable.
The Agrosignal test: Russian agri-software had one company on the market. TD low. But OW=1 (owner wanted to sell), PD=1 (entered cheap), CFD=0 (nobody else hunting). Product → B high. We bought. Old S16 "fragmentation ≥ thousands" would have blocked. New formula passes correctly.

Why these thresholds — geometric mean derivation

Earlier versions used 3,000 as the Hell-Yes threshold — that was anchored on the curated 16-vertical map (Accounting × Doc parsing = 4032, Insurance = 3917, PropMgmt = 3584 — picked so these cells got Hell-Yes). That was anchoring, not first-principles derivation. This version uses thresholds derived from the geometric mean.

The formula S = V × C × A × B with each block 0–10 has a mathematical structure that makes the geometric mean (= "average dimension score") the natural threshold rule:

ScenarioPer-block scoreS = score⁴
Maximum1010,000
All "excellent"96,561
All "good"72,401
All "average"5625
All "weak"381
All "bad"11

If we define Hell-Yes = "all four blocks at least good (≥7)" — meaning no dimension is a weakness — then the threshold is 7⁴ = 2,401. Round to 2,400.

Same logic for other tiers:

  • Strong = all blocks ≥ 5 (average or better) → 5⁴ = 625
  • Conditional = all blocks ≥ 3 (weak but non-zero) → 3⁴ = 81 (round to 100)
  • Pass = geom mean ≥ 1.5 → 1.5⁴ ≈ 5 (round to 20)
  • KILL = at least one block near zero → below 20

Why geometric mean, not arithmetic: the formula is multiplicative. Arithmetic mean would let one strong block compensate for a near-zero block (8+8+8+0 → mean 6). Geometric mean correctly penalizes weak links (0⁴ = 0 regardless of others). This is the right math for "no link in chain is weak".

Why this is better than anchoring: the threshold is now reproducible and defensible. Anyone running the formula on a new dataset gets the same answer about what "Hell-Yes" means. We don't need to recalibrate when the dataset shifts.

Vertical-level verdicts (Layer 1)

For aggregating cell scores up to vertical level:

  • Hell-Yes vertical = ≥2 Hell-Yes cells, OR ≥1 Hell-Yes cell + Σ S ≥ 10,000
  • Strong vertical = max cell ≥ 2,400, OR Σ S ≥ 6,500
  • Conditional vertical = Σ S ≥ 2,400
  • Pass vertical = Σ S ≥ 625
  • KILL vertical = below

Worked example

Insurance Brokerage P&C (NAICS 5242) × "9. Manage Financial Resources"

V = 7V_base 7 (high labor intensity, regulated docs) × workflow_modifier 1.0 (Financial Resources highest AI exposure). LR ≈ $3.6B/yr nationally (claims doc parsing κ=0.95, AR/AP automation).
C = 7π_dist = 0.85 (broker owns client; carrier doesn't). π_moat = 0.85 (50-state license + accumulated book of business). C = 10 × 0.85 × 0.85 ≈ 7.
A = 6t_50 ≈ 1.7 yrs: high adoption pressure (labor savings obvious), moderate friction (compliance), current adoption ~20%.
B = 7tier-2 segment: 145K agencies, low PE saturation (PE prefers >$5M EBITDA), strong silver tsunami, median asking 5.5×, public comps 11×.
S = 7 × 7 × 6 × 7 = 2,058 — Strong 🟢

What's NOT in the formula — and why

Fragmentation as hard-gate
Absorbed into B via TD. Agrosignal one-target case passes the formula. "Fragmentation ≥ thousands" was a 2010s private equity artifact, broken on AI-rollup actionable deals.
CAGR / TAM standalone
CAGR is a metric of static economies. AI changes distribution and cost structure, not market size. TAM is implicit in V_base, but not as standalone variable.
"Profitable at SME level" gate
Absorbed into V. If V > 0, the cell creates extractable value by definition.
"Why this time is different"
AI as external force (Force arrow in Layer 1) answers this directly. If V > 0, AI is rewriting the cell's rules — that is "why now".
Founder access
Not in this formula. About the deal, not the vertical class. Verified when shortlisting top-10 cells.
Geography (US vs EU)
Workflow is the same. Deal-level choice.
Anti-pattern as separate gate
Catchment-bound, asset-heavy, COGS-dominant all mathematically fail on V or B. No separate gate needed.

Honest weaknesses

1. Normalize(V) is subjective. Anchor points may shift as we refine sub-sector profiles.

2. t_50 is estimated. S-curve velocity has ±50% error band.

3. π_moat is a composite. Four moat types compressed into one number. Future version may split.

4. APQC Level 1 is coarse. We use 13 categories for cross-economy screen. For deep-dives, adopt industry-specific APQC PCF (~250 processes) or Level 2 (~70 process groups).

5. Map is static, economy isn't. Recompute quarterly. AI replaceability per APQC category will shift as model capabilities advance.

Working cycle

  1. Quarterly — refresh grid: BLS QCEW labor share, Anthropic Economic Index by occupation, PitchBook PE-saturation snapshot.
  2. Per cell — compute V (V_base × workflow_mod × applicability), C (π_dist × π_moat), A (10/t_50), B (composite).
  3. Compute S — formula applies.
  4. Aggregate to vertical-level verdict — sum + max + Hell-Yes count.
  5. Rank verticals by Σ S — focus on Hell-Yes + Strong.
  6. Top-10 deep-dive — adopt APQC industry-specific PCF for that sector, build P&L bridge, identify named operators, founder access.
