Methodology — Full US Economy

AI Value Migration v3.0 applied to all 20 NAICS sectors at 4-digit level · context-free
What this is. The same formula S = V × C × A × B applied to every NAICS 4-digit industry group in the US economy (~300 entries across 20 sectors: Agriculture, Mining, Utilities, Construction, Manufacturing, Wholesale, Retail, Transportation, Information, Finance, Real Estate, Professional Services, Management, Admin Support, Education, Healthcare, Arts, Food, Other Services, Public Admin). Profiles inferred from public industry data only. No Bobroff Cap operator interviews, no P&L benchmarks, no founder access. The formula naturally filters: ~70% of entries Pass or KILL because they're asset-heavy, government, or commodity-driven; the remaining ~30% are where roll-up math can work.

The formula

Scell = V × C × A × B Cell score = Value Created × Capture Share × Adoption Speed × Buyability

What this answers

Three questions at once:

  1. Does the formula self-validate? If asset-heavy/commodity sectors get correctly killed (B=0 for government, low B for utilities/mining, low V for manufacturing) — formula works at scale.
  2. Are there blind spots? Sub-sectors that score Hell-Yes/Strong here but we haven't researched yet — those go into the deep-dive queue.
  3. Does our curated list match? The 16 verticals in the companion map should score similarly here. Big divergence = either formula or our context needs calibration.

How profiles were scored

Each NAICS 4-digit sub-sector has four base scores (V_base, C, A_base, B) on 0–10, inferred from:

  • V_base — labor cost share × AI replaceability (BLS QCEW + Karpathy.ai/jobs proxies)
  • C — distribution control + license/data moat (industry structure analysis)
  • A_base — current AI adoption % (Anthropic Economic Index by occupation)
  • B — fragmentation × silver tsunami × PE saturation (PitchBook proxies)

Special cases: B = 0 for government, postal service, central bank, holding company management, private households, religious orgs (not acquirable). These automatically KILL via formula.

Score interpretation

Hell-Yes ≥ 3,000 · Strong 1,000–3,000 · Conditional 300–1,000 · Pass 50–300 · KILL < 50.

Honest caveats

1. Profile scores are approximate. Real heterogeneity exists within NAICS codes — for any vertical that screens green, build a focused P&L sheet (like the 16-vertical companion site) before underwriting.

2. Workflow modifiers are universal. Same modifier per workflow across all sub-sectors — refinement needed for sector-specific workflows.

3. No founder access scoring. Hell-Yes here = "deep-dive candidate", not "deal".
